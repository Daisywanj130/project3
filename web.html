Sickle cell Disease(SCD)

Sickle cell is a group of blood disorders caused by mutation in Hb gene

Types of sickle cell crisis

  1. Vaso-occlusive crisis: this is when the sickle-shaped red blood cells block blood flow, causing pain and organ damage
  2. Aplastic crisis: where the bone marrow stops producing enough red blood cells, leading to severe anemia
  3. Hemolytic crisis:occcurs when the red blood cells break down faster than the body can replace them
  4. Acute chest syndrome: it is caused by infection, blocked blood vessels, or inflammation

Management of scd

  • Regular check-ups with healthcare professionals
  • Taking medications to manage pain and prevent complications
  • Staying well hydrated
  • Avoiding extreme temperatures
  • Getting vaccinations to prevent infections

NOTE:Maintain a healthy lifestyle with a balanced diet, regular exercise, and avoiding smoking or exposure to secondhand smoke

Complications of scd

  • Pain crises
  • Anemia
  • Organ damage
  • Delayed growth and development in children
  • Increased risk of infections
  • Stroke
  • Acute chest syndrome
